The program

What is Silkroad Fellows?

Silkroad Fellows brings a small group of university students to Silicon Valley each summer to work directly with early-stage startups. Fellows aren't observers. They ship real work, sit in on real decisions, and leave with a network most students spend years trying to build.

Housing, meals, and a stipend are covered, so the only thing you bring is the work ethic. Run by Silk Road Innovation Hub, the program is fully funded — accepted fellows pay nothing.

Questions

Frequently asked

Is the program really free?

Yes. Accepted fellows pay nothing. Housing, weekday lunches, and a monthly stipend are covered, and we'll reimburse your flight to and from the Bay Area up to $1,500.

Who can apply?

Current university students from anywhere in the world, in any year and any major, who can commit to the full program dates and are comfortable working in English.

Do I need a US visa?

You'll need to be able to legally enter the United States for the duration of the program. We can provide a letter of invitation to support your visa application, but the visa itself is your responsibility.

What kind of startups will I work with?

Early-stage Silicon Valley startups across software, AI, hardware, and consumer products. We match each fellow with a host based on your interests and the team's needs.

Do I need a technical background?

No. We've placed fellows in engineering, design, product, growth, and operations roles. What matters more is that you've built something — a project, a side hustle, a portfolio, a story.

How competitive is the application?

Selective but not impossible. We read every application carefully and care more about drive and demonstrated initiative than résumés or grades.
