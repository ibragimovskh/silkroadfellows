The program

What is Silkroad Fellows?

Silkroad Fellows brings a small group of university students to Silicon Valley each summer to build something real. You can spend the summer working on your own startup with our support, or work on projects within Silkroad Innovation Hub. Either way, you walk out with mentorship, a network, and experience most students spend years trying to build.

Run by Silkroad Innovation Hub, a five-minute bike ride from Stanford and Sand Hill Road. Accepted fellows pay nothing.

Questions

Frequently asked

Is the program really free?

Yes. Accepted fellows pay nothing. Housing, lunch, and travel to the Bay Area are covered, and you'll have access to mentors and workspace throughout the program.

Who can apply?

Current university students based in the United States, in any year and any major. We're prioritizing applicants who are already in the US for the summer — we do not cover international travel or sponsor visas.

Do I need a technical background?

No. We've welcomed fellows from engineering, design, product, growth, and operations. What matters more is that you've already built something — a project, a side hustle, a portfolio, a story.

What does a typical week look like?

Mornings on your project. Afternoons with mentors and workshops. Evenings with the cohort. Plus founder talks, demo sessions, and Bay Area field trips throughout.

How competitive is the application?

Selective but not impossible. We read every application carefully and care more about drive and demonstrated initiative than résumés or grades.
