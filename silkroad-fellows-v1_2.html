The program

What is Silkroad Fellows?

Silkroad Fellows brings a small group of university students to Silicon Valley each summer to build something real. You can spend the summer working on your own startup with our support, or embed with one of our partner companies. Either way, you walk out with mentorship, a network, and a body of work most students spend years trying to build.

Housing is covered, mentors are on hand, and the program is run by Silkroad Innovation Hub. Accepted fellows pay nothing.

Questions

Frequently asked

Is the program really free?

Yes. Accepted fellows pay nothing. Housing is covered for the full eight weeks, and you'll have access to mentors and workspace throughout the program.

Who can apply?

Current university students based in the United States, in any year and any major. We're prioritizing applicants who are already in the US for the summer — we do not cover international travel or sponsor visas.

Do I have to work at a startup?

No. You can spend the summer building your own startup with our support — that's the path we're most excited about. If you'd rather embed with an existing team, we'll match you with one of our partner companies.

Do I need a technical background?

No. We've welcomed fellows from engineering, design, product, growth, and operations. What matters more is that you've built something — a project, a side hustle, a portfolio, a story.

What does a typical week look like?

Mornings on your project. Afternoons with mentors and workshops. Evenings with the cohort. Plus founder talks, demo sessions, and Bay Area field trips throughout.

How competitive is the application?

Selective but not impossible. We read every application carefully and care more about drive and demonstrated initiative than résumés or grades.
